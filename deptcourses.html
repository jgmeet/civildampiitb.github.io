CE324 - Engineering Law

CE324 - Engineering Law


1

Review 1

By Vivek Kumar

Prof. Vandana Bhatt and Prof. Kirty Dave
As engineers we would be involved in lots of contractual work where we would either be offering or accepting various contracts. It becomes imperative to know the laws related to the contracts to safeguard ourselves in case of a dispute. Without proper knowledge we would probably end up losing our licenses without any reason. This course tries to impart the minimum required knowledge of law for a practising engineer and bridge the void that our current curriculum has.
  • Introduction and explanation of various terms eg. Contract, Agreement etc.
  • Formation of Contract and Drafting.
  • Introduction to the Contract Law.
  • Explanation of the various sections of the Contract Law.
  • Types of Contract.
  • Process of Engineering Contract Formation
  • Contract Administration, Shortcomings, and Remedies.
  • General Rules of Interpretation of the Contract
  • Claim- Definition, Identification, and Notification
  • Records- Record Keeping, Sets of Records, Important Records, Managing the records
  • Delays- Cause, Type, Effect
  • Change Item, Extra Item and Variation in Contract
  • Function of Contract Administrators
  • Legal Aspect of Contract Administration
  • Arbitration and Arbitration Law
None
The methodology adopted for the teaching of this course is very different from what is normally adopted in other courses. You are explained a section of the Contract Law or Arbitration Act and it is followed by examples. The slides are just an outline for the course and hence going to the class becomes very important to score well. The professors in general are very friendly and expect you to question and discuss so that you can understand the course in the classroom itself.
This course is fun to learn as you realise what goes on in the disputes which you may have read somewhere in a newspaper or may have to face. This provides for a lot of opportunities for active learning.
The weightage of the exams are:
Mid-Sem: 30%
End-Sem: 50%
Mock Trial: 20%
The examination is closed book and closed notes. The questions are subjective and test both your knowledge of the topics and how well can you implement them in a case given.
Mid-Sem and End-Sem are similar and solving last year papers will help a lot. The Professors expect you to write detailed answers and explain the stuffs in the examination. Discuss with the professors what they would expect this time and answer your paper accordingly.
The Mock trial requires a bit of effort and since it a team trial it is expected that everybody would contribute to the trials and it is the basis for grading the trials.
The course is extremely easy to understand. The examples provided in the class are sufficient to understand the concepts. If classes are attended, which is highly recommended, you do not need to spend time for the self-study. The exmas are also in general quite easy and you can score well in them.
There were no textbooks for the course.

CE403-Design of Structures-III

CE403-Design of Structures-III


1

Review 1

By Abhideep Sahu

Prof. Alok Goyal
The course incorporates more complex designing features of the Civil engineering apart from the usual beams, columns and slabs for the usual Dead and Imposed Loads. DOS III includes the calculation of earthquake and wind load forces (which were not studied in DOS I and DOS II) on structures and then designing of structures for all the applied forces. Apart from these, the course also aims on teaching the designing of bridge deck and gantry girders for the vehicle and crane load respectively.
Design Philosophies for RC structures, Indian code Provisions and their applications, Calculation of Equivalent Earthquake loads and Wind Forces, Gantry Girders, Designing of Bridge Deck (slab) for vehicular load.
Design of Structures I (majorly) and Design of Structures II
DOS III is very helpful for those who are looking forward for the jobs as the design engineer. It explain and teaches the crucial and complex aspects of structural designing which are not taught at the basic levels. Overall course content is quite exhaustive as it cover almost all fields with one problem statement from each field.
Quiz 1 & 2, Midsem and Endsem – all open notes exams. Usually the question types are told prior to exam and almost the same questions are asked with the change in data (values).
Moderately hard – Attending all lectures is always beneficiary. There are certain steps in design procedure which must be understood well or else misunderstanding may lead to a lot of confusion in the exams.
  • Advanced Reinforced Concrete Design by P.C. Varghese
  • Design of Steel Structures by N. Subramanian
This course requires a lot of practice. All the problem statements are told before the exams but they must be solved fully so that any confusion during the exam can be avoided.

CE462-Elements of Structural Dynamics


1

Review 1

By Prajwal.K.A

Prof. R.S. Jangid
It is a very important course not only from a theoretical point of view but also from a practical point of view. It is forms the basis for earthquake engineering. Analysis or design of a structure for wind loads, earthquake loads etc demands the basic knowledge of this course. Structural dynamics is itself currently a very active area of research. Any dynamic analysis of any structure would involve the concepts taught in this course. So, it is one of the most important courses which a structural engineer must do.
Single Degree of Freedom (SDOF) system – Equation of Motion; Generalized SDOF system; Free Vibration; Harmonic Load; Periodic Load; Impulse Load; General Loads; Numerical methods for solving complex differential equations; Multi Degree of Freedom (MDOF) system – Property matrices; Undamped free vibration; Mode superposition techniques.

(Chapters 1-6 & 9-12 from the book “Dynamics of Structures” by A.K.Chopra)
Structural Mechanics I and II. Also, basic concepts of MA 108 are required for solving second order differential equations. So, the professor doesn’t prefer to take second year students.
This is a basic course in the field of structural dynamics. The professor mostly teaches using the book by A.K.Chopra. The course content is a little vast.
The course starts off with the single degree of freedom systems subjected to various types of excitations. After midsem, it goes on to the concepts in multi degree of freedom systems which are very important for practical purposes.
2 Quizzes (10% each), Midsem(30%), Endsem(50%). All the questions are numericals.
The exams are relatively easier. The professor might also give a couple of questions directly from the tutorials. The other problems will also be based on the tutorial problems or the basic concepts taught. But some of the questions towards the end of the course are very lengthy and so beforehand practice is suggested.
Medium
“Dynamics of Structures” by A.K.Chopra.
Grading system is good. He usually wants to pass all the students who have taken this course provided they put in a little amount of effort. As far as AA is concerned, the cut off is around 80.

CE469-Advanced Solid Mechanics

CE469-Advanced Solid Mechanics


1

Review 1 (2013-14)

By Agneya Loya

Prof. Naresh K Chandiramani
This is an honors course meant for higher level understanding of stress and strain as tensors, matrix transformations and related boundary value problems

We covered the following topics with Sir:
Elasticity fundamentals: Stress and strain; Constitutive compatibility, and equilibrium equations, boundary conditions; Types of boundary value problems; Plane stress and strain, 2D problems in rectangular, polar and curvilinear coordinates; Elementary 3D problems; Torsion of non-circular members; Unsymmetrical bending of straight beams, shear center.

The following are other topics listed on asc but it is unlikely you will study them due to time constraints:
Curved beams. Beams on elastic foundation; Thermal stresses; Energy methods: Castigliano’s theorems, statically indeterminate structures; Introduction to viscoelasticity; Introduction to plasticity failure theories, formulation for elasto-plastic problems; Numerical techniques

CE 201 – Solid Mechanics which is a second year core course.

The course is very interesting as well as challenging. It is in fact one of the few courses in the department (until the third year at least) where you’ll be studying a lot of theory and derivations. However, unlike other courses the theory is understandable and easy to put into context because we are familiar with stress and strain. The concepts learned in this course also help a little with third year courses like Structural mechanics.

Prof. Chandiramani demands discipline in class and you may be asked to leave the class if you are sleeping or using the phone or engaging in loud conversations. He only wants interested students to attend the course and has asked distracted students to not come back for multiple classes. This is a 6 credit course but sir likes to teach at a comfortable pace and explains every derivation and problem in detail on the board. Doubts and discussions also take some time. So sir has a policy of taking an extra class (1.5 hours) per week throughout the semester.

The examinations are open books and open notes. You can carry all your handwritten class notes and tutorials to the exam. Sir’s emphasis is on understanding but his exams are not so difficult especially since it is open notes. All exams are numerical based. There is 10% weight age for tutorials which you have to do at home. The tutorials are all the practice you need for the exam and it is a good policy to do them sincerely because you can carry them for exams. Sir discusses the solutions of the difficult tutorial problems in brief before you have to attempt them.

Cheating in exams will definitely result in an FR. Plagiarism in assignments is also penalized.

The course is of medium difficulty. It is difficult to get used to attending 4.5 hours of classes every week but I came to enjoy the experience. The course content is a little difficult but sir ensures you have understood and answers doubts well. As a student you have to be attentive as well. The exam problems are similar to the tutorials and easy to answer with the open notes system.

Going back to the notes before the next class is a good tactic to keep the topics in mind. Consecutive topics are connected and often draw on previous topics. So definitely make good notes and read them once after class to brush up all the new concepts. The extra class hours and detailed explanations in class mean that you don’t have to practice a lot outside class. Doing the tutorials is enough. You can expect an average of one tutorial in two weeks.

Sir’s notes are very comprehensive and it is easy to manage without a textbook. Sir also has all his notes in brief in a PDF version in case you need to check your notes. Ask a D-AMP senior or someone you know who has done the course for these notes.

This is a free PDF that might help – http://www.nptel.ac.in/courses/105106049/pdf-assignments/main.pdf

If you are ready to put in the effort then this course has a lot to offer – detailed understanding of solid mechanics, learning from a very experienced faculty and easy exams.

I was going to pursue my MS in Maritime Engineering and I wanted exposure to higher, graduate level courses from the Ocean Engineering concentration and hence I took CE 707.
Coastal protection works, port and harbors - planning, design and construction aspects; harbor layout, model studies – physical and numerical breakwaters, wharf, jetty, mooring and dolphins, dry docks, shipyards, dredging, CRZ and guidelines, navigation, shipping – ship stability (static and dynamic); submarine pipelines – forces, laying, stresses, buckling; equipments.
None, but doing CE 410 will be very helpful
This course is relatively easy in terms of the content taught, but the difficulty arises due to having to memorise a lot of content. The course is very content-heavy as it deals with key aspects taken into account while designing various different marine structures and their subcomponents. Similar to other design courses, there are various considerations and formulas that are used directly from the code of design of ports and harbours, IS 4651. However, it is much easier to understand why specific terms are incorporated in a formula, and they are easy to remember. Additionally, the design guidelines are easy to understand as to why certain configurations are followed and are designed in that manner. The course is divided into eight modules. Modules 1 and 2 are introductory modules, which act as an introduction to various marine structures in the Coastal, Port and Harbour environment. Modules 3 and 4 deal with the design and layout of ports and harbours and their subcomponents, such as jetties, fenders etc. Module 5 is about various coastal protection methods and talks in great detail about the design of breakwaters and seawalls and the calculation of forces which act on structures in the marine environment. Module 6 dealt with drydocks and their sub-components. Module 7 deals with Soil-Structure Interaction and ground improvement techniques used. Module 8 deals with the maintenance, rehabilitation and repair of marine structures. Unfortunately, the code is not provided during the exams; necessary tables and data are provided. There was a term project as well, which was done in pairs (decided by the professor), for which a report had to be made on a given topic along with a final presentation. The grading policy was as follows:- 2 Quizzes (10% each), Midsem (30%), Endsem (40%) and a Term Project (10%)
Great grading, with ~60% of the class scoring AB and above, 90% of the class scoring BC and above
